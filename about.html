About the Authors

Shy Adelman

Shy Adelman holds a Bachelors of Arts from Sarah Lawrence College and is currently pursuing a Masters of Library and Information Science from Pratt Institute. As an Archivist, her interests include digital legacy planning, personal and artist archives, and preservation of cultural heritage. Aiming to save the history we cannot afford to lose, Shy is dedicated to ensuring accessibility and inclusivity within her archival practices. She currently lives and works in New York City.

Rex Fukuchi

Rex Fukuchi is a UX strategist and designer with a background in agency and startups. As a UX Strategist at creative agency Brooklyn Digital Foundry, he conducted foundational research and created designs to help B2B clients tell their stories through digital experiences. He also contributed as a UX Researcher at BobaTalks, identifying opportunities to support different user groups in finding mentorship. Rex is currently pursuing a masters in Information Experience Design at the Pratt School of Information with the goal of pursuing UX research for digital products. He currently lives and works in Brooklyn.

Jack McKernan

Jack McKernan is a historian and aspiring archivist currently participating in the MSLIS program at Pratt Institute School of Information. He is a graduate of Fordham University with an MA in Modern History. His areas of interest were in urban social movements, covering the Irish Lesbian Gay movement in New York and the later developments in the revolutionary New Left in the context of the heroin crisis of the late 60s and early 70s. His current research focuses on the genesis and maintenance of independent and/or radical archives. He is from Long Island and regrets being obstinately proud of that fact. Jack had a lot of fun getting the JavaScript on this page to work right and looks forward to solving new and more complicated technical problems down the road on his way to becoming an archivist.
